The Real Problem

Fiber in the ground doesn’t generate revenue. Installs do.

Most regional ISPs are excellent at building networks, not at orchestrating high-intent demand and field sales. The result is half-lit service areas, slow payback on capital, and boards asking why take-rates are stuck.

Underperforming internal sales

Inside teams and call centers rarely have the bandwidth or tooling to saturate neighborhoods quickly. Door-to-door is an afterthought, not a discipline.

Low digital lead quality

Generic “fiber in your area” campaigns flood inboxes with unqualified leads that never turn into completed installs or churn out on day 30.

Missed build windows

Crews finish construction, marketing sends a few mailers, then everyone moves on. By the time demand ramps, your best window for dense adoption is gone.

A member-owned electric co-op had invested heavily in fiber construction across three counties. After a year of light-touch marketing, only ~18% of homes passed had converted, and board pressure was increasing.

The Underground designed territories aligned with existing construction phases, stood up a dedicated field team, and launched a hyperlocal digital funnel that pre-qualified and scheduled households into install windows.

Within 60 days, installs per crew more than doubled. Weekly KPI reviews allowed the co-op and Underground to reallocate reps to pockets of high demand, test pricing offers, and tighten targeting around most profitable addresses.

After nine months, the co-op hit a 38% take-rate in priority zones with a clear path to 45%+. Capital payback shortened by several quarters compared to the original business case.
